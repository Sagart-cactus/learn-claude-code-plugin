"We use hexagonal architecture. The DB layer lives in src/infra/. Money is always int64 cents. Never call external HTTP from the domain layer." You type it, the session ends, and tomorrow you type it again. It is the most expensive copy-paste of your week.

Claude Code plugins end that loop. A plugin is an installable bundle that carries your team's conventions as skills, agents, hooks, and live integrations, all loaded before Claude's first response. If Claude Code is the operating system, plugins are the applications. You do not reinstall your editor every time you open a file, and you should not re-teach your assistant every time you open a session.

Takeaway: encode "the way we do things" once, ship it org-wide, and every engineer's AI starts from the same baseline.
