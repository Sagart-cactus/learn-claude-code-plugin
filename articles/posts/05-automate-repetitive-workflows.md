## LinkedIn

You know the task so well your hands move on their own. Copy the diff, type "review this", read the findings, file the comments, update the ticket, run the linter, fix what it flags. Twenty minutes. Now multiply it by every PR, every week, across your whole team.

None of that work is hard. All of it is friction, repeated forever, and that makes it the perfect thing to automate.

A plugin collapses the whole sequence into one invocation. The trick to designing one is to map the "before" in detail first: every manual step you write down is a candidate. Then sort each step into a skill, a hook, or an MCP tool that reaches the outside world. Compose small single-job skills into bigger ones and the leverage compounds.

Takeaway: find the task you resented most this week, write down every step, and that list is your first plugin. The work you repeat is the work worth automating.
