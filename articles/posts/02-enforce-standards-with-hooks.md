Your style guide has a great linter. Someone keeps quietly disabling it.

Every team writes the conventions down, mentions them in review, and watches them erode one "I'll fix it later" at a time. The reason is simple: any standard that depends on a human remembering to apply it will eventually be forgotten.

Hooks in Claude Code remove the human from that loop. A PostToolUse hook matched to your write and edit calls runs your linter the instant code is written, so code that violates your style never survives. The part most people miss: a hook's output flows straight back to Claude, so you do not just fail the build, you hand Claude the exact error and it fixes it. A closed loop, no review step for mechanical checks.

Takeaway: stop asking people to follow the rules. Make the rules enforce themselves.
