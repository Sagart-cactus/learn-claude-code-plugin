## LinkedIn

You can ship a perfect tool and watch your AI never reach for it.

You wire it up flawlessly, you ask for exactly the thing it was built for, and the assistant improvises something else. Nine times out of ten the tool is fine. The problem is one sentence: the description.

Claude does not run your skill because of its name or its folder. It reads the descriptions of everything available and routes the task to whatever fits. "Code review helper" gives it nothing to match against. "Reviews code changes for security vulnerabilities. Use when the user asks to review a PR, audit a diff, or before merging" names the triggers, and that is the whole game. The best descriptions even include negative constraints: the cases where the skill should stay out of the way.

Takeaway: you will spend hours on directory layout. Spend a few minutes on the prose that decides whether any of it ever runs.
